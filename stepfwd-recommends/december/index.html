December releases

December 17, 2025

As 2025 comes to a close, dubstep producers have dropped their final batch of releases. Whether you need last-minute ideas for a musical Christmas gift or weapons for New Year’s USB sticks, there is something for every taste.

A first remarkable release was Kamoun’s psychedelic work featuring dubbed-out foley sampling. This was followed by Beatrice M’s classy debut on Tempa with three delicate wobblers, as well as Henry Greenleaf’s tribute to Croydon’s embryonic dubstep. We also can’t overlook SPE:C’s 10-year anniversary remix compilation. To round things off, we share truant’s latest release: two free-download VIPs of previously released tracks, featuring house-inflected vocals and a garage-leaning feel.

Kamoun - Beetje Brak EP [Black Teeth Records]#

Kamoun harnesses echo-laden tribal drums to drive a powerful half-step pulse. Dense field recordings entwine with cutting-edge bass design across three world-building tracks.



Beatrice M. - Eurotrash EP [Tempa]#

Capping off a prolific year, Beatrice M.’s latest EP veers into wobblier, weightier territory without sacrificing their trademark elegance, driven by filtered synths woven with shimmering vocal samples.



Henry Greenleaf - Ghost Town EP [Dimeshift]#

A tribute to the original Croydon dubstep blueprint—twisting 2-step patterns, stripped-back soundscapes, and evocative film samples coalesce into a sound both historically faithful and innovative.



V/A - SPE:C Remixed 3 [SPE:C]#

Five wobbly remixes from the SPE:C catalogue, exploring 2-step alongside fast and slow mutations of dembow. The release is unified by the label’s signature cold, hybrid sound.



truant - VIPS [self-released]#

Truant gifting two house-leaning vocal VIPs is not to be slept on. Lights Out, one of his darkest dubstep cuts, gets a particularly satisfying flip, repurposed into an uplifting anthem for early morning closings.
